What are GDPR data breach notification requirements?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has specific requirements for notifying the relevant authorities and affected individuals in the event of a personal data breach. According to Article 33 and 34 of the GDPR, organizations must notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach.

The notification to the supervisory authority must include the following information:

The nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected.
The name and contact details of the Data Protection Officer (DPO) or other contact point.
A description of the likely consequences of the breach.
Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects.
In addition to notifying the supervisory authority, organizations may also be required to notify the affected individuals if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms. This individual notification must be provided in clear and plain language and include:

A description of the breach.
The name and contact details of the DPO.
A description of the likely consequences of the breach.
Measures taken or proposed to mitigate the effects of the breach.
It's essential to document all breaches, regardless of whether notification is required. This documentation should include the facts relating to the breach, the effects of the breach, and any remedial action taken. This documentation must be available for review by the supervisory authority.

Failure to comply with the GDPR's breach notification requirements can result in significant penalties, up to ‚Ç¨10 million or 2% of the organization's global annual turnover.

There are some exemptions to the notification requirements, such as:

If the breached data was encrypted and the encryption key was not compromised.
If subsequent measures were taken to render the data unintelligible.
If notification would require disproportionate effort, in which case a public communication may be accepted.
In summary, the GDPR's data breach notification requirements are designed to ensure that organizations take prompt and effective action to mitigate the effects of a breach and protect the rights and freedoms of affected individuals.
